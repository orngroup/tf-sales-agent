Trip Factory
Sales Agent

Travel Answers Group  ·  India Office  ·  Role Framework

Single point of contact
Full-circle ownership
Zero-error accountability

The Trip Factory Sales Agent owns every touchpoint of a booking — from the first enquiry to the final financial close. They are the single, named point of contact for both the end client and the trade agent, and carry full accountability for accuracy, service quality, and financial outcome.

1
Phase 1
Sales & Consultation

Enquiry handling

  • Respond to B2C and B2B leads within agreed SLA
  • Qualify dates, group size, budget & special needs
  • Identify dietary, accessibility & visa requirements
  • Log all enquiries in CRM with full detail notes

Tour recommendation

  • Match client profile to the best-fit itinerary
  • Present guided vs independent options clearly
  • Upsell premium hotels, business class & extras
  • Share branded itinerary PDF / digital proposal

Quoting & negotiation

  • Build accurate cost sheet with correct margins
  • Apply agent commission structures correctly
  • Provide competitive quote with validity period
  • Counter-negotiate without eroding margin floor

Closing the sale

  • Confirm verbal agreement via written summary
  • Collect deposit as per agreed payment schedule
  • Send booking confirmation to client & agent
  • Open booking file in system immediately on deposit
2
Phase 2
Component Booking

Flights

  • Source best fares via GDS / NDC / consolidators
  • Book correct name, passport, meal preferences
  • Add frequent flyer numbers & seat selections
  • Monitor for schedule changes and re-routes

Hotels & accommodation

  • Cross-check availability against all tour dates
  • Book correct room type, meal plan & occupancy
  • Confirm preferences: floor, non-smoking, extras
  • Request early check-in / late checkout if needed

Transfers & ground

  • Book all airport and inter-city transfers
  • Confirm correct vehicle type for group size
  • Coordinate port pick-ups for cruise legs
  • Share driver details with client before travel

Tours, guides & extras

  • Book escorted day tours with licensed guides
  • Pre-purchase entrance tickets — skip-the-line
  • Manage visa invitations & travel insurance
  • Coordinate cruise / rail passes as required
3
Phase 3
Confirmation & Documentation

Supplier confirmations

  • Chase every supplier for written confirmation
  • Verify names, dates & room types against booking
  • Resolve discrepancies before issuing documents
  • Re-confirm all services 72 hrs before departure

Travel document pack

  • Compile vouchers, e-tickets, hotel confirmations
  • Produce day-by-day itinerary in client language
  • Include emergency contacts & local SIM guidance
  • Send full pack at least 10 days before departure

Pre-departure briefing

  • Call client to walk through entire itinerary
  • Confirm passport validity & visa requirements
  • Brief on local customs, weather & dress codes
  • Provide agent's 24/7 emergency contact number

Agent / trade handover

  • Send booking summary to trade agent partner
  • Confirm commission due and payment timeline
  • Provide agent with emergency escalation contact
  • Notify agent of any last-minute changes promptly
4
Phase 4
In-trip & Post-trip Support

Issue resolution

  • Be reachable throughout the client travel window
  • Handle hotel complaints & missed transfers
  • Re-book flights on cancellation immediately
  • Escalate to supplier management where needed

Customer queries

  • Answer client questions on tours, timings, logistics
  • Assist with on-ground restaurant & activity requests
  • Coordinate with local guides for amendments
  • Keep client calm and informed at all times

Agent queries

  • Respond to trade agent queries within 2 hours
  • Provide live status updates on client's tour
  • Relay any on-trip changes back to agent promptly
  • Manage agent expectations professionally always

Post-trip follow-up

  • Welcome client home with personalised message
  • Collect Google / TripAdvisor review actively
  • Gather improvement feedback for the ops team
  • Initiate re-sell conversation for next holiday
5
Phase 5
Financial Accountability

Cost control & margin

  • Build cost sheets with all components itemised
  • Maintain minimum margin per booking type
  • Validate supplier invoices against agreed rates
  • Flag any rate discrepancies before payment

Invoicing & collections

  • Issue correct invoice to client / trade agent
  • Track deposit and balance payment deadlines
  • Follow up on overdue payments within 24 hours
  • Issue receipts immediately on every payment

Supplier payments

  • Ensure supplier payments are made on time
  • Avoid cancellation risk from non-payment
  • Cross-check payment confirmation with booking
  • Escalate urgent payments to accounts team

Zero-error policy

  • Agent carries full accountability for booking errors
  • Double-check all names, dates & costs before confirm
  • Perform final audit checklist 7 days before departure
  • Document all amendments with reasons on file
6
Phase 6
Reporting & Improvement

Performance tracking

  • Log conversion rate on all enquiries
  • Report monthly booking value & margin
  • Track repeat bookings & referrals
  • Review error rate — target zero losses

Knowledge & product

  • Attend supplier webinars & training
  • Keep European product knowledge current
  • Share market intelligence with team
  • Suggest new tours / routes to product

Team collaboration

  • Brief ops team on complex bookings
  • Handover files cleanly when on leave
  • Share learnings from issue resolution
  • Mentor junior agents on booking process
Key competencies required
Destination knowledge European products, routing, visa rules
GDS proficiency Amadeus / Galileo, NDC, booking tools
Communication Client, trade agent & supplier management
Attention to detail Names, dates, costs — always verified
Financial acumen Margins, invoicing & reconciliation
Problem-solving Calm, decisive & solution-focused
