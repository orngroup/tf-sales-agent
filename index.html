Sales Agent Role Framework
India Office  ·  Full-Circle Ownership  ·  Single Point of Contact
7 phases Zero-error accountability Sell · Book · Visa · Confirm · Support · Finance
✈

One agent. One booking. Total ownership.

The Trip Factory Sales Agent is the single named point of contact for both the end client and the trade agent throughout the entire booking lifecycle. While certain specialist tasks — such as flight ticketing and visa processing — are handled by dedicated internal teams or outsourced partners, the agent retains full ownership and accountability for ensuring these are initiated, progressing, and completed correctly and on time. No handoffs. No gaps. No excuses.

1
Phase 1
Sales & Consultation
Enquiry → confirmed booking

Enquiry handling

  • Respond to B2C and B2B leads within SLA
  • Qualify travel dates, group size, budget & all requirements
  • Identify dietary, accessibility and visa needs upfront
  • Log every enquiry in CRM with full detail immediately
  • Follow up every live enquiry — never let it go cold

Tour recommendation

  • Match client profile to the most suitable itinerary
  • Present guided, semi-guided & independent options
  • Upsell premium hotels, upgrades & signature experiences
  • Share a branded day-by-day itinerary PDF or digital proposal
  • Position TripFactory expertise vs competitor offerings

Quoting & negotiation

  • Build a fully itemised cost sheet with correct margins
  • Apply trade agent commission structures accurately
  • Provide a competitive quote with a clear validity period
  • Counter-negotiate commercially without eroding margin
  • Offer tiered options at different price points if needed

Closing the sale

  • Confirm all verbal agreements via a written summary email
  • Collect deposit in line with the agreed payment schedule
  • Issue booking confirmation to client and agent same day
  • Open the booking file in the system immediately on deposit
  • Set balance payment deadline reminders in advance
2
Phase 2
Component Booking
Hotels · Transfers · Tours & Experiences

Hotels & accommodation

  • Cross-check availability against every night of the tour
  • Book the correct room type, meal plan & occupancy exactly
  • Confirm floor, non-smoking, view & special preferences
  • Request early check-in / late checkout where required
  • Add special notes: anniversary, honeymoon, medical needs

Transfers & ground

  • Book all airport, inter-hotel and inter-city transfers
  • Confirm vehicle type is correct for group size and luggage
  • Coordinate cruise port and rail station pick-ups precisely
  • Collect and share driver contact details before departure
  • Provide suppliers with meet-and-greet sign board instructions

Tours, guides & experiences

  • Book all escorted day tours with fully licensed local guides
  • Pre-purchase attraction tickets — skip-the-line where available
  • Confirm guide language matches client's preference
  • Organise cruise, rail or coach passes as required by the tour
  • Arrange travel insurance appropriate to the full itinerary

Booking file & records

  • Maintain a complete, up-to-date booking file at all times
  • File all supplier confirmations against the booking immediately
  • Flag any supplier unable to confirm within 24 hours
  • Record all amendments with date, reason and authorisation
  • Ensure every component is confirmed before issuing invoice
3
Phase 3
Flight Coordination
Booked by flights team · Agent owns the outcome
Agent responsibility

Brief the flights team

  • Submit a complete flight request to the flights team promptly
  • Include passenger names exactly as they appear in passport
  • Specify preferred airlines, routing, cabin class and budget
  • Flag meal preferences, FF numbers and seat requirements
  • State the deadline by which tickets must be issued
Agent responsibility

Follow up & verify

  • Chase the flights team daily until confirmation is received
  • Verify confirmed flight details against the original brief
  • Check names, dates, routing and class are all correct
  • Confirm ticket numbers are on file before releasing to client
  • Monitor for any schedule changes or involuntary re-routes
Agent responsibility

Client communication

  • Communicate confirmed flight details to client promptly
  • Ensure client understands check-in times and terminal info
  • Add e-tickets to the travel document pack before dispatch
  • Action any client-requested amendments through the flights team
  • Escalate pricing disputes or ticketing delays to management
ⓘ
Important: Flights are ticketed by the internal flights team, not by the sales agent. However, the sales agent is fully responsible for initiating the request, chasing for completion, verifying accuracy, and ensuring tickets are in the client's hands before departure. If tickets are wrong or delayed, accountability sits with the agent.
4
Phase 4
Visa Application Management
Outsourced processing · Agent owns the timeline
Agent responsibility

Initiate the application

  • Identify visa requirements at the point of booking confirmation
  • Advise client on all visa categories required for the itinerary
  • Issue a visa invitation letter or sponsorship document if needed
  • Refer client to the approved external visa processing partner
  • Set the application start deadline on the booking file
Agent responsibility

Document collection

  • Provide client with the full document checklist for each visa
  • Chase client for outstanding documents within 48 hours
  • Review submitted documents for obvious errors before forwarding
  • Confirm the visa agent has received and accepted the full set
  • Log the submission date and reference number on the booking file
Agent responsibility

Progress tracking

  • Follow up with the visa company at least twice per week
  • Record each update against the booking in CRM
  • Communicate progress or delays to the client proactively
  • Escalate any concerns to management at the 3-week mark
  • Never assume the visa is progressing without written confirmation
Agent responsibility

Completion & verification

  • Confirm visa has been granted and received by the client
  • Verify visa validity dates cover the full travel period
  • Check number of entries matches the itinerary requirements
  • Add visa confirmation to the pre-departure briefing checklist
  • Escalate any refusal immediately and explore alternative options
⚠
Important: Visa processing is handled by an external partner. The sales agent is accountable for initiating the process on time, tracking progress proactively, and ensuring the visa is granted and verified before the pre-departure briefing. A client travelling without a valid visa is a critical failure — the agent must never assume this is someone else's responsibility.
5
Phase 5
Confirmation & Documentation
Every detail verified before departure

Supplier confirmations

  • Chase every supplier for written confirmation of all services
  • Verify names, dates and room types against the original booking
  • Resolve every discrepancy before issuing client documents
  • Re-confirm all services 72 hours before departure
  • Escalate unresolved supplier issues to management immediately

Travel document pack

  • Compile all vouchers, e-tickets and hotel confirmations
  • Produce a clear day-by-day itinerary in the client's language
  • Include hotel addresses in local script for taxi and navigation use
  • Add emergency contacts, local SIM and currency guidance
  • Dispatch the complete pack minimum 10 days before travel

Pre-departure briefing

  • Call client to walk through the entire itinerary in full detail
  • Confirm passport validity meets destination entry requirements
  • Confirm visa has been received and is correct — check dates
  • Brief on local customs, weather expectations and dress codes
  • Provide agent's direct WhatsApp as 24/7 in-trip emergency contact

Agent / trade handover

  • Send a complete booking summary to the trade agent partner
  • Confirm commission amount due and exact payment timeline
  • Share the emergency escalation contact with the agent
  • Notify agent immediately of any last-minute amendments
  • Ensure agent is confident to respond to their client's questions
6
Phase 6
In-trip & Post-trip Support
Always reachable · Issues owned end to end

Issue resolution

  • Be reachable on WhatsApp throughout the client travel window
  • Handle hotel complaints and room issues immediately and directly
  • Re-arrange missed or failed transfers without any delay
  • Re-book flights cancelled or significantly disrupted
  • Escalate critical issues to the ops team and management

Customer queries

  • Answer client questions on tours, timings and all logistics
  • Assist with on-ground restaurant and activity requests
  • Coordinate with local guides for on-trip amendments or additions
  • Provide local emergency numbers and medical facility guidance
  • Keep the client calm, informed and feeling fully supported

Agent queries

  • Respond to all trade agent queries within 2 working hours
  • Provide status updates on the client's trip when requested
  • Relay any on-trip incidents or changes to agent promptly
  • Handle client complaints relayed via the agent diplomatically
  • Manage agent expectations professionally throughout

Post-trip follow-up

  • Welcome client home with a warm personalised message
  • Actively request a Google or TripAdvisor review within 3 days
  • Gather constructive feedback for the operations team
  • Log supplier performance issues for future reference
  • Initiate the re-sell conversation for the next holiday
7
Phase 7
Financial Accountability
Agent is financially responsible at every stage

Cost build & margin control

  • Build a fully itemised cost sheet for every booking before quoting
  • Maintain the minimum required margin on every component
  • Never issue a quote without completing the full cost build first
  • Review margin impact whenever an amendment is made
  • Escalate to management before accepting below-minimum margin

Client invoicing & collections

  • Issue the correct invoice immediately once booking is confirmed
  • Track deposit and final balance payment deadlines without exception
  • Send automated payment reminders 7 days and 2 days before due date
  • Follow up overdue payments assertively within 24 hours
  • Issue formal receipts immediately on every payment received

Supplier invoice validation

  • Validate every supplier invoice against the agreed contract rate
  • Flag any discrepancy to the supplier in writing before paying
  • Never approve a supplier invoice with incorrect amounts
  • Ensure all supplier payments are processed on or before due date
  • Cross-check payment confirmation number against the booking record

Zero-error & audit policy

  • Agent carries full personal accountability for all financial errors
  • Double-check every name, date, cost and reference before confirming
  • Complete the mandatory pre-departure financial audit 7 days out
  • Document all amendments: date, reason, authorisation, financial impact
  • Financial losses due to agent error are reviewed in appraisal
