Section 02
Ashish Yadav / Supply
The Clear Boundary
This section exists because it has caused confusion. Read it carefully — it defines exactly what Supply does and does not own.

SUPPLY — ASHISH YADAV: WHAT HE DOES AND DOES NOT OWN

✔ WHAT SUPPLY OWNS (Ashish's remit)

✔Rate loading coordination — working with Disha to ensure all rates are in the system
✔Quality & compliance standards — ensuring all suppliers meet ORN standards
✔India supply specifically — hotels, transport, experiences for the India market
✔Sri Lanka — sits under Ashish's commercial remit
✔Framework supplier agreements — review of master terms globally

✘ WHAT SUPPLY DOES NOT OWN

✘Destination product creation — building itineraries, packages or tours for any Contract Manager's destination
✘Destination-specific contracting — if David Tan owns Thailand, Ashish does NOT contract Thailand suppliers
✘GIT loading & contracting — this sits in Product (Sachin for India; Contract Manager for their destination)
✘Marketing or demand creation — this sits within Product
✘Any destination in the ownership table that has a named Contract Manager
In plain English: Ashish ensures the commercial and operational standards across supply. Contract Managers own their destinations completely — they source, contract and build product. These roles are complementary. They do not overlap. If a destination has a named Contract Manager, Ashish has no commercial role in that destination.

Section 04
Key Principles
These are the agreed rules. They apply globally and without exception.
One Owner Per Destination
Every destination has exactly one Contract Manager. If your name is on a destination, you own it completely — contracting, suppliers, product. No one else may touch it.
Supply ≠ Destination Ownership
Ashish Yadav (Supply) secures commercial rates and framework terms for India and overall compliance. He does NOT own, contract or build product for any destination that has a named Contract Manager. See Section 02.
No Overlaps — Ever
Overlaps are not permitted. If two people believe they own the same destination, it is escalated to Ajay Kawa immediately for resolution.
Disha / Mumbai Hub
Disha loads rates and content for ALL destinations globally. She does not own any destination commercially — she is the operational backbone that services all managers.
GIT sits in Product
GIT loading and contracting is a Product responsibility — Sachin for India, the named Contract Manager for all other destinations. It is not a Supply function.
Global Chains & Cruise
Manish Suchde owns global hotel chain and cruise agreements. These are cross-market and complement all destination managers — they do not override destination-specific contracting.
Reporting
All Contract Managers report to Ajay Kawa. Sachin and Ashish also report to Ajay. Disha reports to Ashish / Sachin.
Unallocated Destinations
Any destination marked TBA is unallocated. No one may begin contracting or building product for a TBA destination until Ajay formally assigns it.
